For Lincoln Teen Gabe Johnson, Success Is Looking Crystal Clear

Gabe Johnson, a 17-year-old Lincoln resident, established Vanguard Window Cleaning as a full-time enterprise before completing high school early. He operates the business with a demanding schedule, working up to 11 hours daily, seven days weekly.

"Don't wonder if you could do it. I think all it takes is just acting on it."

Johnson launched the venture three years ago using minimal equipment — just a mop and squeegee. Today, his operation includes a fully equipped truck and multiple employees serving hundreds of clients across Lincoln.

Among his notable clientele is former Nebraska football coach Tom Osborne. Johnson recalled the unexpected nature of working with such a prominent figure: "I hopped out of the shower soaking wet and I called him back" when Osborne reached out.

The young entrepreneur credits parental influence for developing his work ethic. He emphasized his philosophy on perseverance: "You're going to have the bads, you're going to have the falls, but you just got to keep going through them."

He reflects on resilience and character: "The person you are is defined by what you choose to do when you do fall down."
